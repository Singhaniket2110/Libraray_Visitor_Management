About Us

The library which has now become an essential part of the social fabric has been a product of the actual needs of modern civilization.

Culture is essentially a social communication of experience of our predecessors. Books have preserved this experience & the library has been disseminating it to the living individuals for further progress. Each individual has the 'Right to information'. Our library tries to do its best by satisfying the hunger for information.

Navneet College Library was set up in the year 2010. The library in 2018 was named after our mentor "Smt. Kesardevi R. Mishra" and hence is now popularly known as "Smt. Kesardevi Mishra Memorial Library". The motive behind renaming the college library happens to be Smt. Kesardevi Mishra's dedication, commitment and passionately curious nature towards the "Education" even though being only a primary education recipient in her student days but she stood firmly for the cause of "Education" and helped it reach to great heights and our college happens to be a small example of her mammoth efforts. Over the years, it has been flourished in to an up to date system and fully automated library.

The library was operated in a manual manner but in the year 2015 the library was automated. The software used in automation was Khoa. As per emerging needs of students the college decided to migrate the library data to KOHA Library Software in 2021.

Our library has total collection of books 5808, 10 journals & magazines, 04 newspapers & 32 CD-ROMs. The facility of reading room & CD-ROM facility at digital library are made available to the students & teachers. Library also subscribes the N-LIST Program of INFLIBNET. Through N-LIST Program more than 6000+ e-journals and 3135000+ e-books in various subjects is available for the users. Digital library is well-equipped with latest infrastructure of computers, headphones & CDs. Library also provide Book Bank Scheme for economically backward and needy students.

Newspapers are mirror of the world. Important articles from the newspapers are displayed on the notice board regularly. Newspaper clippings file is maintained in the library which provides a good source of references to the students as well as the staff.

It is no doubt that library is the ocean of knowledge & our college strives hard to add more drops of knowledge by increasing number of books, dictionaries, encyclopaedias, yearbooks, atlases, journals, magazines, newspapers & audio-visual materials & make readily accessible to the students for improving their intellectual level.

Library Rules:
  1. The college library is open to members of staff and students from 9.00 am to 4.30 pm. from Monday to Saturday. Library will be closed on Sunday and Public Holidays.
  2. The library provides open shelf facilities.
  3. In order to make use of the library, students are required to produce their identity cards / library cards.
  4. Silence is to be maintained in and around the reading room areas of the library.
  5. Students are requested not to upset the order of arrangement of books in the cupboards.
  6. Smoking, eating and chewing gum in the library are not permitted.
Circulation Section Rules:
  • Reference Books: These include encyclopaedia, dictionaries, atlases, multi volumes, art books etc. which cannot be borrowed.
  • Senior College and Post Graduate Students: Can borrow two books which they can renew one time more.
  • Junior College Students: Can borrow one book which they can renew one time more.
  • Periodicals, files and projects: Are to be used only in the library.
  • Students must, on receiving a book, examine it, and report to the clerk any damage found therein. If they fail to do so, they will be responsible for any damaged that may be detected later.
  • Books lost, damaged or defaced must be paid for by the reader on whose name they were issued. The value of the books will be assigned by the librarian.
  • On the late submission of books fine of Rs. 5/- per day will be charged.
  • Ex-students of the college can become members of the library. No books are allowed for home issue, but they can do reference in the library only. Xeroxing is permitted.
